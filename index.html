Welcome to the Falls Trail

Mullens, West Virginia

Nature’s Architecture

Nestled in the rugged heart of Wyoming County, the Falls Trail offers an intimate encounter with the raw beauty of the Appalachian Highlands. The journey takes you through a landscape shaped by time and water, where the Marsh Fork and Black Fork falls serve as the trail’s twin crown jewels.

The Earth Beneath Your Feet

The local soil here is primarily Gilpin-Dekalb, a stony, well-drained silt loam born from the ancient sandstone and shale of the mountains. This acidic, nutrient-rich earth is the secret architect of the lush environment, providing the adequate foundation needed for our towering hardwoods and dense undergrowth to thrive.

Our Living Flora

As you walk, you are surrounded by a diverse canopy of Sugar Maples, Tulip Poplars, and Eastern Hemlocks. The forest floor is a vibrant mosaic; keep an eye out for the iconic Great Rhododendron, which forms thick, evergreen tunnels along the creek banks.
